Traditional vs. Generative AI

How artificial intelligence has evolved in financial services

📊

Traditional (Predictive) AI

Analyses numbers, schedules, and structured patterns to predict highly-accurate probabilities.

  • Credit Risk Cards: Scoring client reliability
  • Fraud Shield: Spotting irregular card usage in real-time
  • Market Math: Running algorithmic stock trades
Objective: Analysis & Forecasting
✨

Generative AI (GenAI)

Trained on vast text corpora to create entirely original, structured, and coherent natural language content.

  • Cognitive Search: Retrieving details across procedures
  • Code Generation: Boilerplate scripts, queries, and macros
  • Content Synthesis: Summarising audits and client emails
Objective: Creation & Optimization
05 / 21

Understanding Large Language Models

The mechanics of next-token prediction and context windows

⚡

Next-Token Autocomplete

LLMs operate as highly advanced statistical autocomplete engines. They don't 'think'; they calculate the mathematical probability of the next word (or 'token') based on the prompt you input.

📖

The Context Window

Think of this as the model's active, short-term working memory. Our secure Google tools feature massive context windows, letting you upload hundreds of pages of guidelines or legal documents for instant synthesis in one chat.

⚠️

Hallucinations & Integrity

Because outputs are based on statistics, models can generate plausible-sounding falsehoods ('hallucinations'). Verify before using. Human oversight is irreplaceable; you are responsible for the final output.

UniCredit AI Classifications

Our active mapping of regulatory risk tiers inside the banking operations

Strictly Banned

Prohibited Systems

Systems that manipulate behavior or infringe basic human rights. In UniCredit:

  • Workplace Emotion Recognition: AI to track employee fatigue, attention, or stress is strictly forbidden.
  • Social Scoring: Segmenting users based on social profiles.
  • Untargeted Biometric Scraping: Mass harvesting of CCTV.
ExCom & CISO Approval

High-Risk AI Systems

Critical systems influencing life decisions or key controls. In banking:

  • Creditworthiness Evaluation: AI scoring loan or credit risks.
  • Recruitment & HR: Automated resume filtering or job-ad targeting.
  • AML Profiling: Customer profiling for money laundering check.
  • Compliance: Requires a CISO Technical opinion, Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA), and formal Executive Committee (ExCom) sign-off.

The Internal Governance Process

UniCredit AI Process v1.0: Six phases from concept to production

01
Qualification

IT Risk Team classifies if a proposed tool legally constitutes an 'AI System' via questionnaire.

02
Classification

Determines the risk tier (Prohibited, High, Limited, or Minimal) per the EU AI Act.

03
CMDB Registry

Register the system as an 'AI System' in the IT Asset CMDB, assigning Business Impact scores.

04
Assessments

Run IT security audits and Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) under GDPR.

05
Approvals

Limited risk gets CIO sign-off. High-risk requires CISO opinions and formal ExCom approval.

06
Recertification

Annual audit checking accuracy, bias, data drift, and regulatory compliance.

Daily Compliance Checklist

Golden rules for secure and compliant artificial intelligence usage

✓

DO — Play it Safe & Smart

  • Use Secure Tools: Rely strictly on corporate Gemini & NotebookLM when logged into @unicredit.pl.
  • Verify Outputs: Apply active human oversight. Verify code, translation details, and data before sending.
  • Anonymize Inputs: Remove client names, specific IDs, and sensitive secrets. Generalize to "Client X".
  • Stay Competent: Complete mandatory training modules to comply with AI literacy directives.
✗

DON'T — Steer Clear of Violations

  • No Personal Accounts: Never sign into public ChatGPT, Copilot, or personal @gmail.com tools for bank work.
  • No Customer PII: Do not upload sensitive personal data protected by GDPR to AI engines without a formal DPIA.
  • No Shadow Deployments: Do not spin up custom APIs or connect models without IT Qualification.
  • No Prohibited AI: Never attempt to implement biometric profiling or workplace emotion trackers.
